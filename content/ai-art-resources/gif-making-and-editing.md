# What is a GIF?

GIF is a file format that can store multiple images and colors in a single file, allowing for simple animations.

---

# Ways to Make a GIF

1. From Images

> Use multiple still images stitched into a sequence

2. From Video

> Trim a clip into a short loop and export as a GIF.

3. With AI

> Some image generators, such as Midjourney/Niji and SeaArt, to name but two, have built in AI Video Makers, which you can use to animate a photo to a video and download as a GIF.

---

# Tips and Best Practices

When making a GIF, there are a few things to keep in mind.

1. Don’t overdo it

> If it’s too flashy, too fast, or too blurry, it makes people not want to look at it. Try not to give someone an epileptic seizure.

2. Keep the file size small

> GIFs get heavy *fast*. Reduce the quality or resolution and compress it if needed. You can also limit the frame count.

3. Loop smoothly

> Use “ping-pong” or seamless transitions for the best effect.

4. Use text captions sparingly for clarity

> Consider how fast the frame will be changing when using text captions. People lose their patience when they have to re-watch a GIF a million times in order to read something. Keep in mind how small the image will be as well when choosing text size.
