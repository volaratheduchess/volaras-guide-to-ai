Structured Prompting, much like its name, is a more structured way of prompting. It has more rigidity than Basic Prompting, even for all its flexibility. It is built upon the principle of using lists of attributes and keywords rather than full sentences.

Example: “ultra-detailed, fantasy castle, glowing runes, sunrise, high contrast, cinematic lighting.”

![](assets/ai-art-resources-image-prompting-structured-prompting-86.jpg)![](assets/ai-art-resources-image-prompting-structured-prompting-87.jpg)

As you will see shortly, Bing responds best to Structured Prompting as opposed to other methods. With Structured Prompting, it optimizes information for the AI to interpret while reducing the character count, leaving less room for the AI to get confused and more room for it to interpret.

---

# Methods of Prompting

As with anything AI, there are a few different ways of writing these kinds of prompts, especially when there are multiple elements such as the subject (ie. a human, animal, or object) and the background. You can say all the information directly:

> Anime art style, man, short dark hair, blue eyes, tattoos, white t-shirt, jeans, black boots, sitting on a bench.

or you can help the AI understand it by grouping words together:

> Art style: Anime + subject: man, short dark hair, blue eyes, tattoos, white t-shirt, jeans, black boots + pose: sitting on a bench + background:

There are pros and cons to both methods. Grouping it together helps information not be confused and reassign values, but it takes up more character count in the prompt. Directly stating the elements may lead to the AI reassigning values if it isn’t clear, but it is a shorter prompt. They fill each other’s weaknesses.  
  
Now that we’ve got two different methods, let’s create the baseline. Remember, these are meant to be basic prompts, so the results won’t always be ideal. You can always add more tags such as “cute, masculine, muscled, intimidating, beautiful, etc” to influence the image.

# Conclusion

As you can see, the results are all very similar even with the different prompting styles. You can also see the results of the style tags much more clearly than they did when using Basic Prompting, specifically for the Digital Art style. This is a perfect example to show how different sites react differently to different styles. Keep this in mind and don’t get discouraged. Keep experimenting and you will learn.  
  
These techniques are applicable across all sites because it is the input prompt.
