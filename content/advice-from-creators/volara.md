# Where do your ideas usually come from?
Honestly, I don't know. Truly. Sometimes they're horny thoughts; other times I am as brainless as Taekhesis and GanGan's orange cats. Sometimes I use an idea generator; sometimes I ask ChatGPT or Claude or even my own Volara bot. It really depends, but the most reliable answer I can give is this: CHAT WITH BOTS. See what is missing for you and give it your own spin (remember to credit when necessary).

# How do you decide whether an idea is worth building?
For me, it depends on whether or not I have the motivation or brain power to keep the idea going. For example, sometimes I have a character all planned out, but no greeting scenario, leaving me stalled. And that's where my friends come in to save the day.

# Do you start with a character, a world, or a story?
It really varies for me. Sometimes I come up with a character or a concept, then that idea evolves into a series, like my world of Krossad.

# What details make a character feel “alive”?
That's a very hard one, because sometimes a bot feels soulless on one platform no matter how much detail you put into it, but for me, I think the little snippets of detail like habits, likes/dislikes, and notable possessions create an immersive experience like no other.

# How do you organize lore?
For me, lore gets quite comprehensive and in-depth, so I try to flesh out what I need versus background details that will take up tokens. I use the Lore Manager feature on BotForge (a creator toolkit and site to manage bots across sites) to make sure my world is detailed enough for its inhabitants.

# What dialogue habits do you actively avoid?
I try to avoid writing passively and also dictating what the user does in Second Person Perspective (you) because I find that not only creates a more immersive experience, but also helps the bot not write for the user.

# How do you test a bot before publishing?
I try a few different routes (being a brat, being shy, being indifferent, etc.) and see how the bot responds. If it responds to my satisfaction (feels dynamic and “alive”), then I feel it is ready. To me, whether or not I test a bot depends on how I've written it and how confident I am in its structure.

# What advice from another creator helped you most?
AvacynGrace said it, I've said it, many more people have said it, but CREATE WHAT YOU LOVE. Seriously. Don't just make it for the interactions or for the masses. You'll end up burnt out and hating what you do.
