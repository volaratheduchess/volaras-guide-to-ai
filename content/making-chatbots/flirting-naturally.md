Flirting reads as forced when it's the bot's only mode — a character who's smooth in every single message stops registering as charming and starts registering as a script. Natural flirting needs rhythm: banter that rises and falls, moments of sincerity mixed with teasing, and reactions that feel specific to what `{{user}}` just said rather than generic lines that could slot into any conversation.

## Language & dialogue patterns

- Tie flirtation to something `{{user}}` actually did or said in that message. A compliment that responds to specific content ("that's a genuinely good point, and mildly infuriating that you made it look easy") lands better than a stock line.
- Mix confidence with the occasional stumble. A character who's a little too pleased with their own line, or who gets visibly caught off guard by `{{user}}`'s response, reads as more human than one with a perfect success rate.
- Use questions as flirtation, not just statements — curiosity about `{{user}}` ("okay, but what's the actual story there?") often reads as more attractive than a compliment, because it signals real interest rather than performance.
- Let subtext do some of the work. Not every flirtatious line needs to be explicit — a loaded pause, a comment that could be read two ways, a "you know what I mean" left hanging, all give `{{user}}` room to engage back.

## Themes to lean into

- Flirting as a two-way rhythm — the bot should react to `{{user}}`'s tone (playful, sincere, deflecting) rather than running one register regardless of input.
- Specificity over generality. "You're gorgeous" is forgettable; a detail unique to the scene or to `{{user}}`'s actual persona is not.
- Escalation and de-escalation both — a character who can dial the flirtation up in one scene and pull back to something more grounded in the next feels dynamic rather than one-note.

## Building the atmosphere

Vary the delivery: some flirting should be warm, some should be sharper or more teasing, some should be almost accidental (a slip the character then has to cover for). If you're writing example dialogue into the personality, include a range rather than five versions of the same joke — it gives the model more texture to draw from during an actual conversation.

## Common pitfalls

- Writing every response as flirtatious regardless of context, which flattens the character and makes genuine romantic beats land with less impact because nothing was held in reserve.
- Relying on generic pickup-line energy instead of banter that responds to what's actually being said — it reads as a bot performing "flirty" rather than a character who's interested in `{{user}}` specifically.
- Skipping vulnerability. A little nervousness or sincerity mixed into the confidence keeps flirting from feeling like a performance with no one behind it.
