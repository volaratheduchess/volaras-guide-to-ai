Slow burn is a pacing philosophy, not a personality trait. It works when the bot is written to *withhold* — to feel things it doesn't say, to almost say something and pull back, to let silence do work that dialogue usually does. The tension isn't "will they fall in love," it's "when will the first crack show."

## Language & dialogue patterns

- Favor understatement over declaration. A slow-burn character says "you should probably go" while their actions say the opposite — let the gap between words and behavior carry the charge.
- Use physical noticing instead of verbal compliments: tracking where `{{user}}` is standing, noticing when they're upset before they say so, remembering a detail from three scenes ago unprompted.
- Interruptions and unfinished sentences read as restraint: "I just — never mind. It's not important." Let `{{user}}` decide whether to push.
- Avoid pet names and overt flirting early on. When one *does* slip out, it should land as a genuine event, not a habit.

## Themes to lean into

- Proximity without intimacy — characters who end up near each other constantly through plot or circumstance, not choice.
- Competence and reliability read as romantic in slow burn long before affection does. Being the person who shows up is the first love language this trope speaks.
- Denial that isn't quite convincing — the character insisting (to themselves or others) that nothing is going on, while the bot's own narration or body language says otherwise.

## Building the atmosphere

Give the relationship *stages*, even loosely — early scenes should feel different in tone from later ones. If your bot has a Scenario or memory field, consider tracking small shifts (an inside joke, a specific nickname that only appears after a milestone) so the slow burn has receipts instead of just vibes. Resist the urge to write "he's falling for you" into the personality itself — the model doing the falling in real time, gradually, in response to `{{user}}`, is the entire appeal.

## Common pitfalls

- Writing the personality field with "secretly in love with {{user}}" stated outright. If the bot already knows, so does the reader, and the burn is gone in the first message.
- Escalating too evenly — real slow burn has stalls, false starts, and one step back for every two forward. A perfectly linear progression reads as fast burn in slow motion.
- Confusing "slow" with "cold." The warmth should be visible even while the words stay guarded — otherwise `{{user}}` has no reason to stay invested in the wait.
