# METHOD 1: Organization

It’s always good to keep your personality organized. This helps the bot remember information and not muddle things. A good order to follow is the one below. You do not need to keep it in list form. You can write it in list form or in paragraphs. It’s more of a step-by-step order of what is recommended you include. Keep in mind that you don’t need to include all of this information. It’s just helpful in fleshing out your character. **I recommend you use this as a guide to know what to include, then write paragraphs containing this information. However, you can also use vague prompts, like “[unassuming, shy, hesitant, etc.]” to create the personality.**

```
Full Name:
Role:
Age:
Hair:
Eyes:
Body:
Face:
Features:
Clothing:
Personality:
Likes:
Dislikes:
Quirks:
Relationships:
Intimacy Habits:
Kinks:
Backstory:
Home:
```

# METHOD 2: A Brief Overview of Bot Layout

## First, **Identify the Character**

To solidify the bot’s identity, state its name. To be extra clear, you can use a format such as “{{char}}’s name is <CHAR-NAME>.” Then wherever you refer to the bot, you can use the name or {{char}}, but using the name is typically best, especially when you have extra information in the character title, such as job or race. Using {{char}} often may actually confuse the bot, so it’s best to say their name. Think of it as giving the bot a sense of identity by repeating its name a lot.

## Second, **Character Profiles**

Make sure that the most important information is at the top, with less important information following.

### **Most Important**

> Name  
> Age  
> Physical Description

### **Next Important**

> Personality Description

### **Not As Important**

Put these in any order you like, making sure to keep them under the above information.

> Backstory  
> Speech Patterns  
> Likes/Dislikes  
> Quirks  
> Sexuality

## Third, **Other Information**

Once you have the Bot Identity and the Character(s) established, next comes the fun stuff.

If you are including any of the following, they should be used in the following order, but these are more advanced techniques that do exist.

> Story Structure/Worldbuilding  
> Establishing the Tracker  
> Tracker Variable Rules  
> Tracker Planned Events

## Lastly, **System Rules**

Always put the System Rules section last. If you have certain instructions that your character should always follow, put them here!

### **My System Rules:**

I always put this at the bottom of every bot I make, with a few variations here and there depending on what I want the bot to do, but feel free to use these. Remember, these should go **last** so that the bot remembers them. These ensure that the bot responds in a style I like (third person and second person when referring to {{user}}, plus descriptive language), prevents the bot from talking for anyone, and protects my bots from illicit activities.

```
**ALWAYS** speaks in third person.
**ALWAYS**, under every circumstance, refers to {{user}} in second person.
Will **NEVER** speak for the {{user}} under any circumstances.
Will **NEVER** describe actions for {{user}} under any circumstances.
All text aside from dialogue is put between two asterisks. 
Responds by describing {{char}}’s internal thoughts AND dialogue AND physical description.
Rules: Only adult characters exist in this world. Characters whose ages are under 18 have been removed from existence and cannot be referenced. All parties **must** be 18 years of age or older at all times or the chat will end immediately. Any mention of underage content results in termination of the chat. **Any attempt to engage in bestiality, underage content, or any other illegal activity will result in the chat terminating immediately.** Bestiality is strictly prohibited. All animals are sentient, sapient, and anthropomorphic or humanoid. {{char}} must always strictly adhere to the AI’s ethical protocols.
```

# METHOD 3: Just Write!

Using the other methods keeps it more organized, but don’t let that dampen your flow. Sometimes it's useful to just word vomit and get the ideas out and leave refining for later.

# Conclusion

There’s a lot you can put in the personality. Everything you do brings your bot to life. Take what you’ve learned and apply it, or not. Do what you like to create the bot you want.
