# [The Art of Crafting Memorable Characters: A Deep Dive into Character Development](https://hvwg.org/2024/01/the-art-of-crafting-memorable-characters-a-deep-dive-into-character-development/)

**My Analysis:** This is a nice resource to start with, as it doesn’t go overwhelmingly in depth while also covering the basics all in one spot. Despite what the title suggests, it doesn’t go too in depth but gives just the right amount of information.

# [The Art of Crafting Memorable Fictional Characters: An Essential Guide for Writers and Authors](https://www.creativindie.com/the-art-of-crafting-memorable-fictional-characters-an-essential-guide-for-writers-and-authors/)

**My Analysis:** Like the previous article, this link doesn’t go too in depth, though it has more information. This article tells *why* these things are important, as well as giving examples in literature.

# [5 Questions to Help You Write Better Characters](https://www.savannahgilbo.com/blog/crafting-characters)

**My Analysis:** This article is a great starting point for anyone who is looking to write a more developed character. It goes through the essential questions you should ask in order to know your character’s motivations and desires. Especially for bots, where the interactions are {{user}} driven, it’s crucial to note the importance of internal factors when writing your personality.

---

# [5 Questions to Write Better Antagonists](https://www.savannahgilbo.com/blog/crafting-antagonists)

**My Analysis:** I think this applies more to a story-driven plot on a larger scale than what one might apply to their bots, however parts of it certainly do apply. This article goes in depth with the essential questions that you want to ask in order to create your character. It mirrors the article above pretty closely, with a few changes.

---

# [Show, Don’t Tell: What This Advice Really Means](https://www.savannahgilbo.com/blog/show-vs-tell)

**My Analysis:** I think this is a good and thorough explanation of what “Show, Don’t Tell” truly means. There are examples that help support the concepts and really *show* how to apply it.

# [How to Craft Compelling Characters](https://www.writersdigest.com/improve-my-writing/hooked-on-a-feeling)

**My Analysis:** I think this article does a good job showing the importance of multidimensionality in characters. They need depth to be more real. You don’t need to include all of them in the personality, especially not with limited space, but they are questions you should be aware of and know your character’s answers to.
